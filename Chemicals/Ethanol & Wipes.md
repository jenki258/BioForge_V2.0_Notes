#Chemicals
## Overview
In BioForge, re‑usable glassware (Culture Vials) and Syringes can become **contaminated** with dangerous infection strains.  
Ethanol and Wipes are the only items that can safely **remove that contamination** without destroying the container.

- **Ethanol** – a laboratory‑grade solvent. Stackable up to 16.  
- **Wipes** – disposable cleaning cloths. Stackable up to 64.

Both perform the same functions, but Wipes are cheaper and stack higher, making them ideal for field work.

---

## Cleaning Actions

| Target Item | Condition | Result |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| **Dirty Culture Vial** | Always | Vial becomes a clean **Live Culture Vial** |
| **Syringe** | Must be **empty of blood** | Infection strain is removed; syringe becomes safe |
| **Syringe** | Still contains blood | Action fails – message reminds you to empty the syringe first |

---

## How to Use

1. Hold the **Ethanol** or **Wipes** in your main hand.  
2. Hold the **dirty vial** or **empty syringe** in your other hand.  
3. Right‑click - the item is cleaned, and one Ethanol/Wipe is consumed.

If the target is a syringe that still has blood, you will receive a chat warning:  
*“Syringe must be empty of blood first.”*
