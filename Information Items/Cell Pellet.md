#InformationItems 

A **Cell Pellet** is a concentrated biological sample produced by the **Centrifuge**.  
It contains residual blood data and can be analysed in the Microscope to reveal neurological and infection‑strength details.

---

## Obtaining

1. Fill a **Blood Tube** with blood (right‑click a filled needle/syringe on an empty tube).  
2. Place the filled tube in a **Centrifuge** that has a valid recipe for Cell Pellet output.  
3. The centrifuge will process the tube and randomly produce either a **Cell Pellet** or a **Plasma Sample**, transferring all blood data and infection.

---

## Properties

- Stores the same blood data as a Blood Slide: type, source, subject UUID, reagent knowledge.  
- Also carries any infection strain that was on the original blood tube.

---

## Usage

### Microscope
Insert the Cell Pellet into the Microscope. It will display:  
- **Infection Strength** (as a percentage)  
- **Reflex Delay** (as a percentage)

The Calibration mini‑game (five sliders) must be completed before the grid appears.

### Viewing Data
Hover over the Cell Pellet to see:  
- Source name  
- Blood type  
- Reagent results (Anti‑A, Anti‑B, Anti‑D)  

---
## Comparison: Slide vs. Pellet vs. Plasma

| Property | Blood Slide | Cell Pellet | Plasma Sample |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **How to obtain** | Syringe/Needle + slide | Centrifuge (blood tube) | Centrifuge (blood tube) |
| **Infection data** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Microscope symptoms** | Heart rate, temp, infection strength | Infection strength, reflex delay | Infection strength, oxygen saturation |
| **Calibration required** | 4 sliders | 5 sliders | 2 sliders |
| **Reusable** | Permanent record | Consumed when read? No, stays | Same as Cell Pellet |
