#InformationItems 

A **Plasma Sample** is another product of the **Centrifuge**, representing the liquid component of blood.  
Like the Cell Pellet, it can be analysed in the Microscope, but it reveals different symptoms.

---

## Obtaining

Identical to the Cell Pellet – place a filled **Blood Tube** in a Centrifuge. The output is randomly chosen between Cell Pellet and Plasma Sample.

---

## Properties

- Contains blood data (type, source, subject UUID, reagent knowledge).  
- Carries any infection strain from the original blood tube.

---

## Usage

### Microscope
Insert the Plasma Sample into the Microscope. After calibration, it shows:  
- **Infection Strength** (percentage)  
- **Oxygen Saturation** (percentage)

The Calibration mini‑game (two sliders) must be completed first.

### Viewing Data
Hover over the Plasma Sample to see:  
- Source name  
- Blood type  
- Reagent results  

---
## Comparison: Slide vs. Pellet vs. Plasma

| Property | Blood Slide | Cell Pellet | Plasma Sample |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **How to obtain** | Syringe/Needle + slide | Centrifuge (blood tube) | Centrifuge (blood tube) |
| **Infection data** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **Microscope symptoms** | Heart rate, temp, infection strength | Infection strength, reflex delay | Infection strength, oxygen saturation |
| **Calibration required** | 4 sliders | 5 sliders | 2 sliders |
| **Reusable** | Permanent record | Consumed when read? No, stays | Same as Cell Pellet |
